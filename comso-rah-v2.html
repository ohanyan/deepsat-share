Purpose of This Meeting

DeepSat is requesting this meeting to introduce our VLEO ISR program to COMSO, understand how our trajectory aligns with COMSO's commercial integration mission, and explore how to build a direct working relationship — including how COMSO can support our active STRATFI submission (due 29 May 2026) and connect us with relevant PEOs for current and future opportunities.

Who We Are

DeepSat is an early-stage space ISR company building the first dual-use constellation purpose-built for Very Low Earth Orbit (VLEO) — approximately 300 km altitude. At this orbit we collect higher-resolution imagery with superior signal-to-noise characteristics than conventional LEO systems, while operating below the primary debris belt and the most threatening ASAT engagement envelopes.

We are COTS-first by design. Our platform integrates commercially available components from Redwire, Satlantis, Kepler, and NVIDIA, producing an end-to-end ISR service — detections, custody, and analytic output — not a bespoke government program. This is the kind of commercial capability COMSO was stood up to integrate.

Why VLEO Matters to COMSO

COMSO's mission is to integrate commercial capabilities into the Space Force architecture at speed. VLEO is an orbit that no current commercial ISR constellation operates from at scale. DeepSat addresses a genuine gap — and does so using COTS hardware on a commercially financed model, which is exactly the profile COMSO seeks to operationalize.

  • Novel data layer. VLEO produces a class of imagery and sensing data that has not been collected from this altitude in any operational capacity. This is new capability for the architecture, not redundancy.
  • Below the ASAT envelope. VLEO satellites operate below the primary threat envelope for kinetic anti-satellite weapons, offering a more survivable tier for crisis and conflict scenarios — directly relevant to CASR planning.
  • Commercial-first architecture. DeepSat's supply chain is entirely COTS. COMSO does not need to fund DeepSat's development. We are seeking integration pathways, not program funding.
  • Dual-use by design. Our constellation serves both commercial and defense customers. This is the model Col. Trimailo has described publicly as what COMSO is looking for: companies that can survive without a Pentagon contract and therefore deliver on Pentagon timelines.

Operational Engagement to Date

DeepSat has active engagement with defense customers relevant to COMSO's combatant command coordination mandate:

  • SOCOM — TE 26-2. DeepSat is participating in SOCOM Technical Experimentation 26-2 at Avon Park Air Force Range, demonstrating our ISR platform directly with special operations end-users.
  • SOUTHCOM / JIATF-S. Counter-narcotics S&T engagement with Joint Interagency Task Force South, validating our maritime detection capability against an active operational demand signal.
  • AFRL — D2P2 SBIR. Awarded a $1.25M Direct-to-Phase II contract from AFWERX/SpaceWERX — our primary non-dilutive funding vehicle and a direct pathway into the SpaceWERX/COMSO ecosystem.
  • MDA SHIELD IDIQ. DeepSat is prime on the MDA SHIELD IDIQ, with a $151B ceiling vehicle providing a contracting pathway for missile defense-adjacent sensing work.

What We Are Asking from COMSO

We are not asking for program funding. We are asking for COMSO's guidance and introductions to ensure we are integrating correctly into the Space Force's commercial framework.

  1. Understand COMSO's mission alignment. We want to hear directly from COMSO where VLEO ISR fits in the commercial architecture — and how to structure our engagement to be useful to COMSO's mandate.
  2. Build a working relationship with COMSO. We see COMSO as a long-term partner as our constellation grows. We want to understand how to stay engaged, contribute to CASR framework development, and be part of the TacSRT vendor ecosystem.
  3. STRATFI guidance (due 29 May 2026). COMSO does not sign STRATFI letters, but COMSO can help us identify and connect with the right mission PEO signatory — whether PEO Space Sensing, PEO-TIS, or PEO-SDA — for our active submission.
  4. PEO connectivity — current and future. Beyond STRATFI, we want to understand how to develop relationships with Program Acquisition Executive offices as they evolve, ensuring DeepSat is positioned for future task order and acquisition opportunities.
