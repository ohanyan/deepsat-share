The May 2025 USSF-NGA roles MOU clarifies the seam: USSF runs the sensors, NGA runs the analysis. DeepSat is built to be the sensor layer USSF owns.

3. Three Scenarios, One Platform

Three scenarios shaped to SSC's stated mission areas: TacSRT, Resilient MW-MT, and Space Domain Awareness. Each scenario engages the same three layers of the platform: the VLEO sensor, the on-orbit intelligence and decision layer, and the orchestration / publish path into Joint Force C2.

Scenario 1TacSRT Custody Handoff in INDOPACOM
A combatant command requires sustained pattern-of-life on a contested EEZ where adversary vessels routinely spoof AIS and rendezvous between national-system passes. DeepSat's VLEO satellites accept the tasking through GDM, run multi-modal fusion (optical, SWIR, polarimetry, AIS) on orbit, and emit detection events, AIS-mismatch, dark-vessel rendezvous, sanctions-evasion patterns, directly into the Joint Force C2 fabric and to NGA-aligned exploitation under the May 2025 USSF-NGA roles MOU. No analyst sees raw pixels; the Joint Force sees a decision.
Intelligence & decision layer: on-orbit object-based production (~200 ms inference), polarimetric glare-defeat for dark vessels, AIS-correlation engine with spoof detection, agentic publish to GDM and downstream C2 in <30 sec.
Scenario 2Dim-Target Under-Layer for Resilient MW-MT
During the schedule window where MEO Epoch 1 is still maturing (Spring 2027 first launch per current SDA reporting), SSC needs commercial complementary cueing for hypersonic and dim-target events. DeepSat's polarimetric VLEO sensors look up at adversary launch and post-boost regimes from below, fuse SWIR signatures with optical and RF on board, and emit cued tracks to MW-MT exploitation and BMC3 in seconds. The decision layer correlates with national assets and stops false positives at the edge.
Intelligence & decision layer: SWIR + polarimetric + optical fusion for low-contrast / dim signatures, on-orbit track formation, OISL relay of cued events to ground without waiting on a station pass, BMC3-shaped publish contract.
Scenario 3Space Domain Awareness from VLEO
SSC's SDA mission area, now scaling under the Andromeda IDIQ, needs more eyes on the LEO and MEO regimes. DeepSat's satellites in VLEO observe upward through clean dark space, against the deep-space backdrop adversary spacecraft live in, with better SNR for small objects than ground stations and free of weather. The decision layer flags unannounced maneuvers, RPO, and pattern-of-life anomalies and publishes them as events to SSC's SDA Mission Area and CASR partners. Raw passes stay on orbit; analysts and warfighters see decisions.
Intelligence & decision layer: non-Earth imaging from VLEO, anomaly detection over spacecraft pattern-of-life, on-orbit track-to-track correlation with national catalogs, agentic / MCP-compatible publish to SDA partners.
What makes all three scenarios possible. A VLEO sensor layer, an on-orbit intelligence and decision layer (multi-modal fusion + object-based production + agentic orchestration), and a publish path that emits events directly into TacSRT, BMC3, and SDA workflows. The same three extend to environmental monitoring, GPS-denied tactical comms, and Joint Force experimentation.

5. The Service: Live Data and Intelligence API

Designed to integrate with complementary sensing systems and external analytical workflows.

1
Customer subscribes to an anomaly type on the Notification Center (e.g. dark-vessel rendezvous, GPS-spoofed track, thermal anomaly over AOI).
2
Onboard GPU runs detection in orbit. A match generates a detection event: anomaly type, compressed packet, full-data reference ID.
3
Event sent in real time over TCP/IP through the LEO backhaul mesh. No waiting on a ground pass.
4
Notification Center publishes the event to every subscribed customer system.
5
Customer system acts on the notification, automatically, agentically, or routed to an operator via existing C2.
6
If deeper context is needed, customer calls the Data Engine REST API with the reference ID.
7
Data Engine retrieves full data, full-resolution frame, adjacent frames, sensor-fusion slices, from onboard storage in real time.
8
Customer takes follow-on action on the enriched data: interdict, cross-cue, alert partner force.
Why this matters for SSC's mission portfolio. New sensors (polarimetric, RF, SIGINT, hyperspectral, non-Earth imaging) come in through the payload abstraction layer without changing the API. The same Pub-Sub contract that alerts on a contested-EEZ vessel alerts on a hypersonic post-boost track or an unannounced spacecraft maneuver. Reserved capacity for SSC means reserved anomaly types, reserved tasking, reserved compute, on the precursor and on the production constellation.

  • Dim-target sensitivity. Polarimetric + SWIR fusion enhances low-contrast signatures (cold-boost, post-burnout, glint-defeating coatings) that conventional MWIR may miss.

  • Survivability. Below the principal ASAT envelope, below the LEO debris belt, RF-hardened path via OISL.
The under-layer is integrated through the same payload abstraction layer as our maritime and SDA payloads. New MW/MT-specific sensors integrate without bus or AI redesign; new track-formation models update on orbit.

Appendix B

Optical inter-satellite links for resilient sensing

DeepSat satellites carry Kepler-supplied optical inter-satellite link (OISL) terminals aligned with SDA's optical comms standards. Detection events, cued tracks, and tasking messages flow through the LEO mesh without waiting on a station pass and without traversing RF-vulnerable links.

Why this matters for SSC
  • Latency. Events reach the Joint Force in seconds, not the next ground pass. TacSRT cueing benefits directly.
  • RF-resilient path. In contested environments where adversary jamming or spoofing degrades RF links, the OISL mesh remains operational.
  • Mesh redundancy. Every satellite is a sensor and a router, no single-point ground dependency, graceful degradation under contested conditions.
  • SDA-aligned. Terminals follow SDA's optical comms standards, supporting cross-vendor mesh participation as the architecture matures.
OISL is part of the same payload abstraction the rest of the platform uses, the same publish contract pushes events whether they originate from optical, polarimetric, RF, or SDA sensors.
