Who We Are

DeepSat is creating a new strategic layer of space infrastructure.

DeepSat is building Orion's Belt, an ISR constellation in Very Low Earth Orbit (VLEO) at 250 km altitude that drives rapid decisions for defense and national security operators. The operator receives decisions, not raw imagery. Onboard AI generates detections in approximately 200 milliseconds, and Kepler optical inter-satellite links deliver intelligence to operators in under 30 seconds end-to-end.

We are COTS-first by design. Our platform is built around commercial systems: bus manufactured by Redwire, optical payload by Satlantis, onboard GPU by NVIDIA, laser inter-satellite communications via Kepler, and an environmental-data payload via NASA Goddard collaboration. Together producing an end-to-end intelligence service delivered on a commercially financed model.

Why VLEO Matters to COMSO

COMSO's mission is to integrate commercial capabilities into the Space Force architecture at speed. VLEO is an orbit that no current commercial ISR constellation operates from at scale. Orion's Belt addresses a genuine gap in the architecture, on a commercially financed model.

The product rests on three pillars:

  1. Speed. Onboard AI detection in approximately 200 ms. Sub-30-minute revisit. Sub-30-second end-to-end latency from collection to operator alert. DeepSat is always connected to the internet via Kepler optical inter-satellite links.
  2. Quality. Multi-modal sensing (optical, RF, AIS, polarimetric) with roughly 4x SNR advantage over LEO from VLEO look angles. Sub-meter VNIR and SWIR imaging with multi-band real-time video.
  3. Resilience. Self-cleaning orbit below the debris belt and below the principal anti-satellite (ASAT) threat envelope. COTS-abstracted supply chain. On-orbit model updates.

  • SOUTHCOM / JIATF-S. Counter-narcotics S&T engagement with Joint Interagency Task Force, South, validating our polarimetric maritime detection capability against an active operational demand signal (Q3 2025 onward).
  • DAF, D2P2 SBIR. Executing a $1.25M Direct-to-Phase II contract from AFWERX, our primary non-dilutive funding vehicle and a direct pathway into the SpaceWERX and COMSO ecosystem.

  • NASA Goddard. Letter of Intent for first-of-kind VLEO atmospheric and space-weather datasets.

What We Are Asking from COMSO

Our PY26.1 STRATFI submission is due 29 May 2026, and we appreciate COMSO taking this meeting on short notice. We are not asking for program funding. Our primary ask below is time-sensitive; the remaining three are parallel pathways that support it.

  1. Champion our STRATFI Government Funding Letter ($30M matching funding target, PY26.1 submission, due 29 May 2026). The cleanest path is COMSO championing within SSC, either by signing directly or by introducing us to the right mission PEO signatory. The most relevant PEOs connected to COMSO for VLEO ISR are: PEO Space Sensing, PEO Tactical Intelligence and SATCOM, and PEO Space Domain Awareness.
  2. Commercial-integration on-ramps. Three specific pathways:
    • CASR vendor pool. Path into the first CASR tranche (cohort September 2026). VLEO ISR fits the crisis-surge construct directly.
    • TacSRT pilot inclusion. DeepSat produces tactically timed, unclassified commercial data, the exact product TacSRT moves through the Global Data Marketplace, plus a VLEO and onboard-AI layer the pool lacks today.
    • Working Capital Fund pathway. How a commercially financed provider structures pricing and recurring orders through the WCF construct.
  3. PEO connectivity, current and future. Beyond STRATFI, building relationships with Program Executive Offices as they evolve, positioning DeepSat for future task order and acquisition opportunities.
