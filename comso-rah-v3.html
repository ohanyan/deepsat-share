About DeepSat

Creating a new strategic layer of space infrastructure.

DeepSat is building Orion's Belt, an ISR constellation in Very Low Earth Orbit (VLEO) at 250 km altitude that drives rapid decisions for defense and national security operators. The operator receives actionable intelligence, not raw imagery. Onboard AI generates detections in milliseconds, and in-space backhaul delivers intelligence in under 30 seconds from capture to operator.

DeepSat is COTS-first by design. The platform is built around commercial systems: bus manufactured by Redwire, optical payload by Satlantis, onboard GPU by NVIDIA, laser inter-satellite communications via Kepler, and an environmental-data payload via NASA Goddard collaboration. Together producing a dual-use end-to-end intelligence service.

Why VLEO Matters to COMSO

COMSO's mission is to integrate commercial capabilities into the Space Force architecture at speed. VLEO is an orbit that no current commercial ISR constellation operates from. Orion's Belt addresses a critical gap in the space infrastructure that defense, national security, and global trade depend on.

The product rests on three pillars:

  1. Speed. Onboard AI detection in milliseconds. Sub-30-minute revisit, with proliferation through COTS enabling higher revisit rates and rapid redeployment as the constellation scales. Sub-30-second end-to-end delivery from collection to alert to operator. DeepSat is always connected to the internet.
  2. Quality. Multi-modal sensing (optical, RF, AIS, polarimetric) with roughly 4x SNR advantage over LEO from VLEO look angles. Sub-meter VNIR and SWIR imaging with multi-band real-time video.
  3. Resilience. Self-cleaning orbit below the debris belt and below the principal anti-satellite (ASAT) threat envelope. COTS-enabled supply chain. On-orbit model updates.

Operational Engagement to Date

DeepSat has active engagement with defense and national security customers relevant to COMSO's role in combatant command coordination:

  • JIATF-S/SOUTHCOM. Counter-narcotics S&T engagement with Joint Interagency Task Force, South, validating DeepSat's polarimetric maritime detection capability against an active operational demand signal (Q3 2025 onward).
  • SOCOM, TE 26-2. Participated in SOCOM Technical Experimentation 26-2 in April 2026, demonstrating DeepSat's agentic and cross-cueing capabilities with special operations end-users. Maritime TE follow-on planned August 2026.
  • DAF, D2P2 SBIR. Executing a $1.25M Direct-to-Phase II contract from AFWERX, DeepSat's primary non-dilutive funding vehicle and a direct pathway into the SpaceWERX and COMSO ecosystem.
  • MDA SHIELD IDIQ. Prime on the MDA SHIELD IDIQ, a $151B ceiling vehicle covering missile warning, missile tracking, missile defense, and space-based defense work.
  • NASA Goddard. Letter of Intent for an environmental and space-weather sensing payload to fly on the entire Orion's Belt constellation, generating first-of-kind VLEO atmospheric and space-weather datasets.

Trajectory and Next Milestones

DeepSat's PY26.1 SpaceWERX STRATFI submission is due 29 May 2026. A precursor satellite is scheduled to launch Q4 2027 on a SpaceX rideshare, achieving TRL 8 pre-launch through a series of technology maturation steps and TRL 9 in the operational space environment via the flight itself. From there DeepSat reaches initial operational capability in 2028 and scales toward a target 500-satellite constellation operational by 2030 for global coverage. The scale-out is AOR-prioritized, sequenced for USINDOPACOM, USSOUTHCOM, USCENTCOM, and allies.

What DeepSat Asks from COMSO

DeepSat's PY26.1 STRATFI submission is due 29 May 2026, and the team is grateful to COMSO for taking this meeting on short notice. The primary ask below is time-sensitive; the remaining two are parallel pathways that support it.

  1. Fund DeepSat directly through PY26.1 STRATFI. $30M government matching funding target, due 29 May 2026. Provide a STRATFI Government Funding Letter.
  2. Champion DeepSat within SSC and Space Force. If COMSO is unable to fund directly, champion DeepSat within SSC by introducing signatories that can fund. The most relevant offices for VLEO ISR are PEO Space Sensing, PEO Tactical Intelligence and SATCOM, and PEO Space Domain Awareness.
  3. Commercial-integration on-ramps. Three specific pathways:
    • CASR vendor pool. Path into the first CASR tranche. VLEO ISR fits the crisis-surge objective directly.
    • TacSRT pilot inclusion. DeepSat produces tactically timed, unclassified commercial data, the exact product TacSRT moves through the Global Data Marketplace, plus a VLEO and onboard-AI layer the pool lacks today.
    • Working Capital Fund pathway. Guidance on how to structure pricing and recurring orders through WCF.
