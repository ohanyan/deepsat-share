Appendix: Polarimetry

Polarimetry: detecting vessels that hide

Electro-optic films enable polarimetric imaging, detecting material and surface differences invisible to normal cameras. Vessels hiding from conventional surveillance become visible.

Capabilities
  • Sea-surface target enhancement via Stokes vectors
  • Submarine wake detection (Kelvin wake patterns)
  • Material and surface discrimination
  • Active R&D with helicopter experiments underway

Partnership Deep Dive

Redwire: why this partnership changes the game

Redwire Space
Satellite bus partner. $44M DARPA VLEO contract holder.
Prime contractor, startup flexibility
Redwire is a publicly traded defense prime with the agility to move at startup speed. We get scale manufacturing, flight heritage, and government credibility without the bureaucracy of legacy primes.
DARPA VLEO workshop match
Redwire was awarded $44M by DARPA specifically for VLEO mission design (Nov 2025). DeepSat and Redwire were matched through the DARPA VLEO workshop, validating technical alignment at the highest level.
Path to scale
Redwire has manufactured 100+ spacecraft. Their production line can scale from precursor to full constellation without switching vendors. One partner, PDR through full production.
Why VLEO requires new propulsion
The drag problem
At 200-300 km, residual atmosphere creates significant drag. Satellites lose altitude daily without active propulsion. Traditional chemical thrusters burn through fuel in weeks. Sustaining orbit in VLEO requires continuous, efficient electric propulsion.
Orbion Ion Thrusters
Hall-effect electric propulsion. 3+ year operational life in VLEO. Low thrust, continuous burn maintains orbit at 250-300 km.
Future: Air-breathing electric propulsion (~150 km)
At ultra-low altitudes (~150 km), air-breathing thrusters collect atmospheric particles as propellant. No fuel limits. Indefinite orbit sustainment. This unlocks the closest possible orbit to Earth for persistent surveillance.
Redwire + Orbion + DeepSat AI = the only integrated VLEO-native stack with a path from demonstrator to full constellation.
