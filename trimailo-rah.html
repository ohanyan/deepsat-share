BLUF

DeepSat is a production-contracted, VLEO space-ISR company purpose-built to be exactly the kind of commercial provider COMSO was stood up to recruit: commercially-financed, multi-mission, standards-based, and crisis-surge capable. We understand COMSO is a unity-of-effort office, not a mission PEO — so we are asking COMSO for the three things that sit squarely in COMSO’s authorities: (1) a path into the CASR vendor pool (Sept 2026 first tranche), (2) inclusion in the TacSRT pilot / Global Data Marketplace expansion, and (3) the Working Capital Fund pathway for VLEO-ISR as a commercial service. Separately, we are pursuing a STRATFI Government Funding Letter from the appropriate mission PEO (likely PEO-SN, PEO-TIS, or PEO-SDA) and would welcome COMSO’s brokering / sponsorship to the right PEO signatory.

ALIGNMENT WITH COMSO PRIORITIES

COMSO’s charter — unity of effort for commercial integration across SSC, operationalizing CASR by Sept 2026, standing up the Working Capital Fund, and running the TacSRT pilot across combatant commands — maps directly onto what DeepSat is already building.

  • Commercial-first by design. DeepSat sells an end-to-end intelligence service (alerts, custody, analytic output — not raw imagery). Hardware-abstracted COTS supply chain (Redwire, Satlantis, Kepler, NVIDIA) means COMSO is buying capability, not a bespoke program.
  • CASR-ready surge capacity. The same VLEO architecture serving commercial and allied customers during peacetime can be activated for DoW priority during crisis — the CASR model in practice, on infrastructure COMSO doesn’t have to fund separately.
  • TacSRT-native. Real-time maritime, dark-vessel, and land-based detection with tip-and-cue into TAK-compatible C2. Already delivered against SOUTHCOM / JIATF-S counter-narcotics demand and SOCOM TE 26-2. Fits cleanly alongside the existing TacSRT vendor pool but adds a VLEO/onboard-AI layer that pool doesn’t currently have.
  • Hybrid architecture contributor. VLEO fills a tier the current architecture lacks: below the debris belt, below the ASAT threat envelope, with ~4× SNR advantage over LEO for most EO/IR tasks. Makes the overall SDA/ISR architecture more resilient, not more redundant.
  • Moves at the speed of relevance. From pre-seed (Q2 2024) → AFRL D2P2 win → MDA SHIELD prime in under 24 months. Precursor launch ~18 months out.
