BLUF

DeepSat is building Orion’s Belt, a surveillance constellation in very low Earth orbit (~300 km) that drives rapid decisions for defense and national security operators. Onboard AI makes detections in ~200 ms; Kepler optical backhaul delivers actionable intelligence to the operator in under 30 seconds. Multi-modal sensing (optical, polarimetric, RF, AIS) on a payload-abstracted bus, with an agentic tasking interface. The operator receives decisions, not raw imagery.

We are prime on the Missile Defense Agency (MDA) $151B SHIELD IDIQ. We are executing an active Department of the Air Force Direct-to-Phase II SBIR (X25.5 CSO #FA864925P0298, $1.25M), just wrapped Technical Experimentation with U.S. Special Operations Command (TE 26-2) and hold a NASA Goddard Letter of Intent for first-of-kind VLEO datasets. Our precursor mission is scheduled to launch on a SpaceX rideshare in Q4 2027, target TRL 7.

We understand COMSO is a unity-of-effort office, not a mission Program Executive Office (PEO). For this meeting, we are asking COMSO for the three things that sit squarely in COMSO’s authorities: (1) a path into the CASR vendor pool, (2) inclusion in the TacSRT pilot, and (3) the Working Capital Fund pathway for VLEO surveillance as a commercial service. Separately, we are pursuing a STRATFI Government Funding Letter from a mission PEO and would welcome COMSO’s brokering to the right signatory.

OUR ASK (Aligned to COMSO Authorities)

  1. CASR on-ramp. The clearest path into the first CASR tranche COMSO is contracting (Sept 2026 first tranche per public reporting). Orion’s Belt was designed for the CASR construct: the same VLEO architecture serves commercial and allied customers in peacetime and surges to U.S. Department of War priority during crisis, on infrastructure COMSO does not have to fund separately.
  2. TacSRT pilot inclusion. DeepSat produces tactically timed, unclassified commercial intelligence, the exact product TacSRT was built to move through the Global Data Marketplace to combatant commands. We are already engaged with U.S. Southern Command (counter-narcotics, via Joint Interagency Task Force South) and U.S. Special Operations Command (TE 26-2) under the same data-as-a-service model.
  3. Working Capital Fund pathway. How does a commercially financed provider like DeepSat structure pricing and recurring orders for surveillance-as-a-service through WCF? We are built as a subscription, not a box.
  4. STRATFI brokering (secondary). Endorsement or introduction to the appropriate mission PEO (PEO Space Sensing, PEO Tactical Intelligence and SATCOM, or PEO Space Domain Awareness) as the STRATFI Government Funding Letter signatory for our PY26.1 submission, 29 May 2026 deadline.

WHO IS DEEPSAT

Orion’s Belt, the constellation. Three pillars define the product:

  • Speed. Onboard AI detection in ~200 ms. Under-30-minute revisit. Under-30-second end-to-end latency from collection to operator alert.
  • Quality. Multi-modal fusion (optical, polarimetric, RF, AIS) with roughly 4x SNR advantage over LEO from VLEO look angles. Sub-meter VNIR and SWIR imaging with multiband real-time video.
  • Resilience. Self-cleaning orbit below the debris belt and below the principal anti-satellite (ASAT) threat envelope. COTS-abstracted supply chain (Redwire, Satlantis, Kepler, NVIDIA). On-orbit model updates.

Architecture. Payload-abstracted, agentic VLEO platform. New sensors attach through the abstraction layer without changing the operator-facing API, AI stack, or bus design. NVIDIA IGX Orin 500 onboard compute. Kepler inter-satellite optical backhaul with TCP/IP end-to-end across the mesh, so every satellite is both sensor and router. Agentic, MCP-compatible tasking. Chain-of-custody traceability inherited from regulated-industry heritage (Vineti).

Where we are. Founded 2024, Los Angeles. $2.45M private + $1.25M non-dilutive raised. 10-person team. CMMC-2 demonstrated to Air Force Research Lab. MDA-sponsored Facility Clearance in progress.

What we are executing.

  • Prime on MDA SHIELD IDIQ ($151B ceiling)
  • DAF Direct-to-Phase II SBIR (X25.5 CSO #FA864925P0298, $1.25M, executing)
  • NASA Goddard Letter of Intent for first-of-kind VLEO atmospheric and space-weather datasets
  • USC ISI STTR with Space Systems Command (SF25D-T1201)
  • U.S. Special Operations Command Technical Experimentation (TE 26-2)
  • U.S. Southern Command counter-narcotics demand signal via Joint Interagency Task Force South

TRL. Component payloads at approximately TRL 6 to 8 from prior flight heritage. Integrated system at TRL 5 (breadboard validated, AI detection demonstrated on vessel and cloud imagery). Q4 2027 precursor launch on SpaceX is intended to achieve TRL 7 (system prototype demonstrated in an operational environment).

Leadership. CEO Nerses Ohanyan (Stanford M.S. Mechanical Engineering, SVP at Vineti, prior 8x exit, Viki to Rakuten). CTO Hayk Martirosyan (led Armenia’s first satellite, Hayasat-1).

ALIGNMENT WITH COMSO PRIORITIES

COMSO’s charter, unity of effort for commercial integration across SSC, operationalizing CASR, standing up the Working Capital Fund, and running the TacSRT pilot across combatant commands, maps directly onto what DeepSat is already delivering.

  • Live intelligence to the operator. Raw imagery never leaves the spacecraft. The operator receives decisions: vessel detected, custody handed off, drone tasked. This is the COMSO model in practice. COMSO buys capability, not a bespoke program.
  • CASR-ready surge capacity. Same VLEO architecture, two postures: commercial and allied customers in peacetime, surge to U.S. Department of War priority during crisis. The recurring commercial revenue base sustains the infrastructure, so CASR activation is additive, not the sole sustainment.
  • TacSRT-native. Tactically timed, unclassified commercial intelligence with tip-and-cue into TAK-compatible C2. Already delivered against U.S. Southern Command and Joint Interagency Task Force South counter-narcotics demand and U.S. Special Operations Command TE 26-2. Adds a VLEO and onboard-AI layer the existing TacSRT vendor pool does not currently have.
  • Hybrid architecture contributor. VLEO fills a tier the current architecture lacks: below the debris belt, below the ASAT threat envelope, with roughly 4x SNR advantage over LEO for most electro-optical and infrared tasks. Makes the overall architecture more resilient, not more redundant.
  • Speed of relevance. Pre-seed (Q2 2024) to AFRL Direct-to-Phase II win to MDA SHIELD prime in under 24 months. Precursor launch ~18 months out.
